# Dedication

To the chefs before me who were battle-tested, figuratively and literally — blood, sweat, and tears.

To the chefs before *them*, and the chefs before *them*, whose cumulative knowledge and passion for the craft made everything that came after possible. I never met most of you. You taught me anyway, through every chef who taught the chef who taught me.

To everyone in a kitchen who has missed the holidays, the birthdays, the funerals, the special moments — given up in dedication to humility, humbleness, and the lessons the work teaches you when you stay long enough to be taught. The cost of the apron is real, and I have paid it, and I know what it cost the people who paid it before me.

---

To the night on fish station when I was going down. My mentor — twenty years now, followed alongside through five-star resorts — told me to step aside and let someone else take the lead.

> *"No, Chef. I got this."*

In my head: *I'm V&A. I don't know how to quit.*

I didn't quit. I dug out. The fundamentals held. That night taught me something I am still learning: the brigade is the foundation, but the chef at the station is the one who has to stand up.

---

To Chef Killingsworth at Lucky 32, way before V&A was even in my horizon. He called me out for wearing an executive chef jacket I had not yet earned. I told him the truth:

> *"I know I don't deserve it. It's a constant reminder of who I want to be."*

He let me wear it. That's a different kind of teaching, and I have not forgotten it.

---

To my wife. You did not just light a fire under me when I got complacent — you brought the entire Carnival from Brasil. You knew when to push, when to push harder, and when to remind me what I was building this for. None of this exists without you.

To my sons, **Lucca Morrison** and **Nicholas Morrison**. You had to miss me at important moments. I am sorry, and I am also grateful, because you are the reason I refused to wait for the system to change. I built the foundation so that fewer fathers, fewer mothers, fewer chefs — fewer anyones — have to choose between their craft and the people they love.

---

I did not just write software. I waited for the system to change. It did not. So I redesigned it.

I want to be the best out of the gates when I pull my toes up to the line. I want to force the competition to change how they build theirs. I can't do this alone — it will take all the companies, all the operators, all the brigades, working in their own kitchens to their own standards, to make the industry better.

Years have been lost because we loved the passion of the craft. We gave up things we cannot get back. This platform is the drop in the pond that gives us some of that time back. I hope the ripples through time reach your shores.

---

The Back of House is not the guest interaction. The guests don't see us. They never see us. But without us, Front of House cannot do what they do best.

This is for them — Front of House, who carry the room, who hold the moment, who deliver the experience the guest remembers — and it is also for us. The ones in the back, doing the work nobody applauds.

We see you. We are you.

To the brigade.

---

*— William Morrison*
*EchoAurion · LUCCCA*
*May 2026*
