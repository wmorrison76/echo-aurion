## What Makes This Dictionary "Master Level"

Each term includes comprehensive knowledge across multiple dimensions:

### 1. **Comprehensive Definition**
- Complete, authoritative explanation
- Culinary significance and importance
- Context and relevance to modern cooking

### 2. **Usage Context**
- **Primary usage:** Main application in cooking
- **Secondary usages:** Alternative applications
- **Context:** Where and when this term is used (professional kitchens, fine dining, home cooking, etc.)

### 3. **Category Classification**
Each term is tagged with 1-3 categories:
- **Technique** - Cooking methods and skills
- **Ingredient** - Foods, spices, components
- **Method** - Cooking approaches and procedures
- **Equipment** - Tools and cookware
- **Theory** - Culinary science and principles
- **Cuisine** - Regional and cultural traditions
- **Safety** - Food safety and sanitation
- **Service** - Hospitality and dining service
- **Tradition** - Cultural and historical cooking practices

### 4. **Etymology & Linguistic History**
- **Origin language** - French, Italian, Japanese, etc.
- **Original word** - The word in its source language
- **Original meaning** - What it meant in the source culture
- **Period** - When it entered culinary usage (centuries, decades, modern era)
- **Cultural context** - Which culinary tradition developed it

### 5. **Practical Applications**
- **Primary application** - What chefs use it for most
- **Examples** - Concrete cooking examples
- **Classic dishes** - Iconic dishes that use this technique/ingredient

### 6. **Related Terms**
- Connected culinary concepts
- Prerequisites to understanding this term
- Extensions and variations

### 7. **Historical Context**
- **Period** - When it was developed (19th century French cuisine, ancient Asian cooking, etc.)
- **Culture** - Which culinary culture developed it
- **Significance** - Why it matters in culinary tradition

### 8. **Mastery Level**
Each term is classified by required knowledge level:
- **Fundamental** - Essential basics (mise-en-place, knife cuts, basic techniques)
- **Intermediate** - Professional cooking knowledge (classical sauces, braising, stock-making)
- **Advanced** - Specialized techniques (molecular gastronomy, sous-vide, advanced pastry)
- **Expert** - Master-level knowledge (rare specializations, ancient techniques)
- **Master** - Culinary authority (complete mastery, teaching knowledge)

### 9. **Confidence & Authority**
- Confidence score (0-1) indicating authority of the definition
- Primary sources (Escoffier, CIA, Larousse Gastronomique, etc.)
- Professional standards and certifications
