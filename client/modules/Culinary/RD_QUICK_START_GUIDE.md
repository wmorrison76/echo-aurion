## 3. Interpreting the Metrics

### Key Metrics Explained

| Metric | What It Means | Target | Interpretation |
|--------|---------------|--------|-----------------|
| **Success Rate** | % of experiments reaching ready/deployed | 75%+ | Shows effectiveness of R&D process |
| **Avg Days to Ready** | Days from ideation to approval | <60d | Indicates pipeline velocity/efficiency |
| **Cost Reduction** | Avg % cost reduction per recipe approved | 3-8% | Direct financial impact of R&D |
| **Deployment Rate** | % of ready experiments going to service | 60%+ | Shows confidence in approved recipes |
| **Active Contributors** | Team members with active experiments | Growing | Indicates team engagement/capacity |
| **Supply Risk Level** | Risk of ingredient shortage/price spike | None | Critical to procurement planning |
| **Local Sourcing %** | Percentage of ingredients sourced locally | 60%+ | Sustainability & supply chain resilience |

### Color Coding

- 🟢 **Green (✓)**: Target met or exceeded
- 🟡 **Amber (△)**: Approaching target or warning
- 🔴 **Red**: Below target or critical alert

## 4. Taking Action Based on Dashboard

### If You See...

**High Success Rate (>80%)**
→ ✅ R&D process is effective  
→ 🎯 Continue current methodology  
→ 📈 Consider scaling team/resources  

**Low Success Rate (<70%)**
→ ⚠️ Investigate failing experiments  
→ 🎯 Review gate criteria (too strict?)  
→ 💡 Check for skill gaps or resource constraints  

**Citrus Showing High Cost Variance**
→ ⚠️ Supply risk detected  
→ 🎯 Action: Lock in supplier contracts  
→ 💡 Alternative: Find substitute ingredient  

**Upcoming Experiment Overdue**
→ ⚠️ Timeline at risk  
→ 🎯 Check for blockers (equipment? approval? data?)  
→ 💡 Escalate if critical to menu launch  

**Team Overloaded (Few contributors, many experiments)**
→ ⚠️ Capacity constraint  
→ 🎯 Redistribute work or hire  
→ 💡 Prioritize high-impact experiments  

**High Waste Recovery Rate (>85%)**
→ ✅ Sustainability goal achieved  
→ 🎯 Document and celebrate success  
→ 💡 Use in marketing/positioning

## 6. Common Questions

### Q: Why does my dashboard show zeros?
**A**: You need experiments in the system first.
- Go to Workbench tab
- Create new experiments
- Metrics will populate automatically

### Q: How often do metrics update?
**A**: Metrics recalculate whenever an experiment changes (real-time).
- Edit an experiment → metrics update instantly
- Change experiment status → charts refresh
- Add collaborator → team metrics update

### Q: Can I customize the metrics?
**A**: Yes! See RD_DASHBOARD_IMPLEMENTATION_GUIDE.md for:
- Adding new metrics
- Changing target thresholds
- Customizing colors/styling
- Creating team-specific views

### Q: Why is an ingredient marked "Critical"?
**A**: Volatility tier based on price history:
- **Stable**: <10% annual variance
- **Moderate**: 10-25% variance
- **High**: 25-50% variance
- **Critical**: >50% variance + limited supply

### Q: How are trends calculated?
**A**: Metrics compare current period to historical average:
- 📈 ↑ = Improvement (good)
- 📉 ↓ = Decline (needs attention)
- → = Stable (baseline)
