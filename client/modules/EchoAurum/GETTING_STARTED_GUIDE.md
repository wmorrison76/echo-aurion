## First 24 Hours

### Step 1: Complete Your Profile (15 minutes)

1. Click your name in the top right → **Profile**
2. Complete your profile information:
   - Full name
   - Email address
   - Role (Accountant, Manager, Controller, etc.)
   - Department
   - Phone number (optional)
3. Review your **Permissions** to see what you can do
4. Save changes

**Why it matters:** Your profile helps the system enforce permissions and route approvals correctly.

### Step 2: Review Your Role (10 minutes)

Go to **Profile** → **Permissions** and answer these questions:

- **Can I post journal entries?** (Usually "Yes" for accountants)
- **Can I approve invoices?** (Usually based on amount limits)
- **Can I view reports?** (Usually "Yes")
- **Can I close months?** (Usually "No" unless you're a controller)

**If you need more permissions:** Ask your manager to request in Settings → User Management

### Step 3: Understand the Console (15 minutes)

1. Click **Console** to go to the main dashboard
2. Spend 5 minutes exploring each module in the left sidebar:
   - **Outlet Manager** - Your properties
   - **Multi-Outlet P&L** - Profitability view
   - **GL Journal Entry** - Where you post entries
   - **AP Invoice Manager** - Where you process invoices
   - **Approvals Queue** - Where you approve requests
3. Bookmark your most-used modules

**Pro tip:** Use keyboard shortcut (⌘K on Mac, Ctrl+K on Windows) to search for features

### Step 4: Watch the Welcome Video (10 minutes)

- Go to **Help** button → Watch "Welcome to EchoAurum" video
- This gives you the 30,000-foot view of what the platform does

**First Day Complete! ✓**

## Week 1: Foundation

### Goal

Understand the fundamentals and be ready to post your first transaction by Friday.

### Monday: Chart of Accounts

**What:** Your master list of GL accounts

**Action items:**

1. Go to **Settings** → **Chart of Accounts**
2. Search for these common accounts:
   - 1000 (Cash)
   - 4100 (Room Revenue) or 4200 (Food Revenue)
   - 5100 (Labor)
   - 5300 (Supplies)
3. Write down 5-10 accounts you'll use frequently
4. Ask your controller which accounts require a cost center

**Time:** 20 minutes

**Why it matters:** You'll use these account numbers when posting entries. Know where to find them.

### Tuesday: Cost Centers

**What:** Departments or locations for tracking spending

**Action items:**

1. Ask your manager: "What cost centers do I use?"
2. Examples: Kitchen, Front Desk, Las Vegas Property, etc.
3. Go to **Settings** → **Cost Centers** and find your cost centers
4. Note which GL accounts require a cost center

**Time:** 15 minutes

**Why it matters:** Some accounts won't let you post without a cost center. Know which ones.

### Wednesday: Double-Entry Bookkeeping

**What:** The foundation of all accounting (every entry has two sides)

**Action items:**

1. Read help article: "Posting Journal Entries"
2. Study three examples:
   - Paying an expense (Debit Expense, Credit Cash)
   - Recording revenue (Debit Cash, Credit Revenue)
   - Recording a payroll accrual (Debit Expense, Credit Payables)
3. Practice: Write out 2-3 entries (don't post yet)

**Time:** 30 minutes

**Why it matters:** Every entry you post must have debits = credits. Get this right.

### Thursday: Guardian AI Overview

**What:** Your AI safety system (Argus, Zelda, Phoenix, Odin)

**Action items:**

1. Read help article: "Understanding Guardian AI"
2. Watch video: "How Guardian Protects Your Data"
3. Key takeaway: Guardian checks every entry you post
   - Argus validates compliance
   - Zelda detects duplicates
   - Phoenix detects fraud
   - Odin logs everything
4. Remember: Green = good to post, Red = fix the issue

**Time:** 25 minutes

**Why it matters:** Guardian is your co-pilot. Trust it. If it flags something, investigate.

### Friday: Post Your First Entry

**What:** Post a simple transaction

**Action items:**

1. Find a simple transaction (e.g., "Paid $500 for supplies")
2. Identify the accounts:
   - Debit: 5300 Supplies Expense
   - Credit: 1000 Cash
3. Go to **Console** → **GL Journal Entry System**
4. Click **New Entry**
5. Post the entry
6. Review Guardian feedback
7. Click **Post**

**Success criteria:**

- Entry posts successfully
- Guardian shows all green checks
- Entry appears in your GL ledger

**Time:** 20 minutes

**Celebrate! 🎉 You've posted your first entry!**

## Week 2-4: Operations

### Week 2: Process Your First Invoices

**Goal:** Become comfortable with the invoice workflow

**Action items:**

1. Ask AP manager: "What invoices should I process?"
2. Go to **Console** → **AP Invoice Manager**
3. Process 5 invoices:
   - Review invoice details
   - Check Guardian validation
   - Assign to GL account
   - Click "Submit" (it may auto-approve or go to your manager)
4. Review Zelda alerts for duplicates
5. Ask your manager if you don't understand a line item

**Time:** 60 minutes

### Week 3: Practice Approvals

**Goal:** Approve invoices and journal entries from others

**Action items:**

1. Go to **Console** → **Approvals Queue**
2. Review 5 approval requests:
   - Read Guardian feedback
   - Verify GL account is correct
   - Check cost center is assigned
   - Review supporting documentation
   - Click Approve or Reject
3. Document your reasoning in comments

**Time:** 45 minutes

### Week 4: Your First Month-End Close

**Goal:** Understand the closing process

**Action items:**

1. Work with your controller on month-end
2. Follow the "Month-End Close Checklist":
   - Post accruals
   - Post depreciation
   - Generate Trial Balance
   - Generate Income Statement
   - Investigate variances
   - Reconcile bank accounts
3. Ask questions! This is a learning month.

**Time:** 4-8 hours (spread across the month)

## Quick Reference

### Common Tasks

#### Posting a Journal Entry

1. Console → GL Journal Entry System
2. Click "New Entry"
3. Fill header (date, reference, description)
4. Add line items (debit and credit)
5. Verify debits = credits
6. Review Guardian feedback
7. Click "Post" or "Submit for Approval"

#### Processing an Invoice

1. Console → AP Invoice Manager
2. Click "New Invoice" or "Process Invoice"
3. Fill vendor, invoice number, amount
4. Add line items (account and amount)
5. Review Guardian feedback (duplicates, validation)
6. Click "Submit for Approval"

#### Approving a Transaction

1. Console → Approvals Queue
2. Click on pending item
3. Review Guardian feedback
4. Check GL account and cost center
5. Click "Approve" or "Reject"
6. Add comment (brief explanation)

#### Viewing Reports

1. Console → Reports
2. Select report type (Trial Balance, Income Statement, etc.)
3. Choose period/date
4. Click "Generate"
5. Review for unusual items
6. Export to PDF/Excel if needed

#### Finding an Article

1. Console → Help (bottom of sidebar)
2. Use search bar
3. Select category
4. Click on article
5. Read and bookmark useful ones

### Common Questions

**Q: I'm not sure which GL account to use**

- A: Ask your manager or controller. Don't guess. Wrong account = wrong reporting.

**Q: Guardian is blocking my entry. What do I do?**

- A: Read Guardian's error message carefully. It tells you exactly what's wrong. Fix the issue and resubmit.

**Q: I see a Zelda duplicate warning**

- A: Investigate! Have we already posted this invoice? If yes, reject. If no, document why and approve.

**Q: How do I get approval for this transaction?**

- A: It depends on your role and amount. Go to Settings → Approval Workflows to see the rules.

**Q: I need more permissions**

- A: Ask your manager to request additional permissions in Settings → User Management

**Q: Can I post to prior months?**

- A: Usually yes, within 30 days. After that, ask your controller to reopen the period. After audit, periods close permanently.

**Q: I made a mistake. Can I delete the entry?**

- A: No. Post a reversing entry (opposite amounts), then post the correct entry. Guardian creates an audit trail of both.
